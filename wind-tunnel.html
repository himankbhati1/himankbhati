Subsonic Wind Tunnel

PROJECT ADVISOR: DR. PUNNAG CHATTERJEE

Objective: To design and validate a low cost subsonic wind tunnel for the undergraduate aerodynamic laboratory at IIT Dharwad.

The primary challenge was to achieve professional-grade flow uniformity while adhering to strict budget constraints. This required a rigorous analysis of design trade-offs regarding the contraction cone, test section, and diffuser geometry.

04. Methodology

Analytical Design

Estimated airflow, pressure variations, and operating conditions through analytical calculations. This established the baseline dimensions for the fan and motor selection.

Geometry Refinement

Refined the wind tunnel geometry specifically the contraction cone curve and diffuser angle to ensure uniform flow and efficient performance.

Fabrication & Assembly

Constructed the tunnel using wood for the contraction/diffuser and acrylic for the test section to allow optical access. Implemented flow conditioners (honeycomb) to manage turbulence.

05. Conclusion

The fabricated wind tunnel successfully demonstrates controlled subsonic flow. The tuft visualization tests confirm that the honeycomb straighteners and contraction cone design effectively laminarize the airflow entering the test section, making it a viable tool for undergraduate aerodynamic experiments.
